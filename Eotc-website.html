Eastern Orthodox Christianity

A rich history and faith preserved through thousands of years

What is Eastern Orthodox Christianity?

Eastern Orthodox Christianity is a branch of Christianity that traces its roots to the early Christian Church and is centered in the Eastern Mediterranean region. It is one of the most unique religions out there and stands out from broad Christianity, having been preserved through several thousand years of history.

Church History Before the Bible

This goes deep into the church's history without the Bible, just the Old Testament. The church was there before the Bible was created. For example, St. Paul's letter to the Galatians was written in 48-49 AD, but the gospel was made later in 64 AD. Despite only having the prophets and the Old Testament, the church was fruitful and multiplied abundantly.

The Nicene Creed

The Orthodox Faith believes in the Nicene Creed, which is a clear statement of their faith overall.

We believe in one God, the Father Almighty, Who created heaven and earth and all things seen and unseen. We believe in one Lord, Jesus Christ, the Only-Begotten Son of God, begotten of the Father before all ages. Light from Light, true God from true God, begotten not created, of one essence with the Father, by Whom all things were made...

The 5 Pillars of the Faith

The Orthodox Faith believes in 5 core concepts of their faith:

1. Mystery of the Trinity — There is one God but in three persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. They are all one in three.
2. The Incarnation — God became man in the person of Jesus Christ.
3. The Church — The one holy, universal, and apostolic Church.
4. The Sacraments — The sacred mysteries of the faith.
5. The Resurrection — The resurrection of the dead and life in the world to come.
